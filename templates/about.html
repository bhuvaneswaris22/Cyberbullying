Cyberbullying

  • Cyberbullying involves using digital communication to harass, intimidate, or harm others.
  • It can occur through social media, texts, emails, and online forums.
  • Effects of cyberbullying can include emotional distress, social isolation, and even physical harm in extreme cases.

Types of Cyberbullying

  • Harassment: Sending offensive messages or threats repeatedly.
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone else to damage their reputation.
  • Cyberstalking: Continuously following and harassing an individual online.
  • Outing: Sharing personal information or secrets about someone without their consent.

Cyberbullying Helpline

  • If you are a victim of cyberbullying, you can contact local law enforcement or seek help from trusted adults.
  • There are helplines and support services available to provide assistance and guidance.
  • Take steps to protect yourself online and report cyberbullying incidents to the platform or service provider.
